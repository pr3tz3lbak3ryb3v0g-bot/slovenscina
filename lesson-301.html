· enota · učim se slovenščino
say hello, name things, introduce people
Every Slovene noun has a gender — moški spol (masculine), ženski spol (feminine) or srednji spol (neuter). It usually shows in the ending: a bare consonant is mostly masculine (učitelj), -a mostly feminine (učiteljica), -o/-e mostly neuter (okno). The verb biti ('to be') is irregular — learn the singular by heart. And 'my' agrees with the gender: moj (m) · moja (f) · moje (n).

text

· say hello, name things, introduce people
Živjo! Jaz sem Ana. Sem učenka in se učim slovenščino. To je moja šola. Moja učiteljica je gospa Kovač. Ona je zelo prijazna. To je moj sošolec Marko. Midva sva prijatelja. Rada hodim v šolo.
show English
Hi! I'm Ana. I'm a pupil and I'm learning Slovenian. This is my school. My teacher is Mrs Kovač. She is very kind. This is my classmate Marko. The two of us are friends. I like going to school.
