An A2-level paper modelled on the Center za slovenščino basic-level exam: a personal email, a visit to the doctor, A2 grammar, an invitation to write, and three speaking tasks. The real exam needs 60% per part for A2 (85% for B1).

01

Reading

· Branje
Read Maja's email to her friend, then answer the questions.
Draga Ana, kako si? Jaz sem zelo dobro. Prejšnji teden sem se preselila v novo stanovanje v Ljubljani. Stanovanje je veliko in svetlo, ima tri sobe in velik balkon. V bližini sta park in trgovina. Vsak dan hodim v službo s kolesom, ker ni daleč. Upam, da me kmalu obiščeš! Lep pozdrav, Maja
show English
Dear Ana, how are you? I'm very well. Last week I moved into a new flat in Ljubljana. The flat is big and bright, it has three rooms and a big balcony. Nearby there's a park and a shop. Every day I go to work by bike, because it isn't far. I hope you'll visit me soon! Warm regards, Maja
Kaj je naredila Maja prejšnji teden?
What did Maja do last week?
✓ preselila se je — “… sem se preselila v novo stanovanje.”
Koliko sob ima stanovanje?
How many rooms does the flat have?
✓ tri — “… ima tri sobe …”
Kako gre Maja v službo?
How does Maja get to work?
✓ s kolesom — “… hodim v službo s kolesom …”
Kaj je v bližini?
What is nearby?
✓ park in trgovina — “V bližini sta park in trgovina.”

Bonus — grammar practice

· Raba jezika
Choose the correct form for each gap.
ℹ️ Not a part of the real exam — the official test checks grammar inside the reading, writing and speaking. Extra targeted practice.
Jutri ___ celo knjigo.
Tomorrow I'll read the whole book. (perfective)
✓ preberem — perfective → completed/future
Ta avto je ___ od mojega.
This car is bigger than mine.
✓ večji — comparative: velik → večji
Na mizi je pet ___.
There are five books on the table. (genitive plural)
✓ knjig — 5+ → genitive plural (knjig)
___, prosim!
Speak, please! (imperative)
✓ Govori — velelnik: govori!
Rad ___ kavo.
I'd like a coffee. (conditional)
✓ bi — rad bi = I'd like
Kako ___ imaš?
How are you? (reflexive)
✓ se — imeti se → kako se imaš?
04

Writing — reply to an ad

· Pisanje/ 10
You saw this ad in the newspaper. Write a short letter (40–50 words) to the company: say the work interests you, and ask about the location, the working hours and the pay. Begin and end the letter appropriately.
EVROSAD d. o. o. Iščemo pomoč pri obiranju sadja. Vsak dan od avgusta do oktobra. Možnost dogovora za delo izven sezone.
show English
EVROSAD ltd. We're looking for help picking fruit. Every day from August to October. Off-season work possible by arrangement.
Write your letter to Evrosad.
  • Begin appropriately (e.g. Spoštovani …).
  • Say the work interests you.
  • Ask about the location, the working hours and the pay.
  • End appropriately and sign your name.
show a model answer
Spoštovani, v časopisu sem videl vaš oglas za delo pri obiranju sadja in zelo me zanima. Prosim, povejte mi, kje je delo, kakšen je delovnik in koliko je plačilo. Hvala za odgovor. Lep pozdrav, Tomaž NovakDear Sir/Madam, I saw your ad for fruit-picking work in the newspaper and I'm very interested. Please tell me where the work is, what the working hours are and what the pay is. Thank you for your reply. Kind regards, Tomaž Novak

05

Speaking

· Govorjenje
1. Tell us about last weekend — what you did (use the past tense).
show a model answer
Prejšnji konec tedna sem bil doma. V soboto sem kuhal kosilo in bral knjigo. V nedeljo sem šel na sprehod in obiskal prijatelja.Last weekend I was at home. On Saturday I cooked lunch and read a book. On Sunday I went for a walk and visited a friend.
2. Role-play: you're at a restaurant. Order a starter, a main course and a drink, and ask for the bill.
show a model answer
Za predjed bi juho, za glavno jed pa ribo s krompirjem. Za pijačo kozarec vode, prosim. Na koncu: Račun, prosim!For a starter I'd like soup, and for the main course fish with potatoes. To drink, a glass of water, please. At the end: The bill, please!
3. Give your opinion: Zakaj se učiš slovensko? (Why are you learning Slovenian?)
show a model answer
Slovensko se učim, ker živim v Sloveniji in želim govoriti z ljudmi. Jezik je težak, a zelo zanimiv.I'm learning Slovenian because I live in Slovenia and want to talk to people. The language is hard but very interesting.
